Selected Research

Research

Computational reasoning

Do error rate, evidence relation, and social setting impact dependency reasoning by people and Bayesian Networks?

This project compares human judgments with Bayesian Network predictions in reasoning from dependent and independent information sources. The experiment tested whether error rate, evidence relation, and social setting shape probability updating in corroborative and contradictory evidence contexts. Model comparison suggests that participants' information updating was insufficient relative to Bayesian Network predictions.

Representational analysis

Representational similarity analysis for recategorizing university majors in a high-tech 21st-century context

This work adapts representational similarity analysis to behavioral and text-derived data. The project builds an NLP pipeline over a 300-million-word original corpus to quantify 21st-century competencies and produce a more interpretable structure for university majors.

Social cognition and AI

The conformity of a Chinese new generation born in the AI era under the setting of an artificial intelligence "liar"

This project adapts the Asch conformity paradigm to test whether individuals conform to judgments made by an AI agent. The study asks how trust, disagreement, and social pressure shift when the confederate is artificial rather than human.

Causal learning

Causal inferences contribute to biased learning, predicted by a novel Bayesian model

This project investigates people's causal inference process during biased learning with Bayesian models and reinforcement learning models. The work applies Bayesian modeling to describe and predict biased learning in sequential decision settings.
